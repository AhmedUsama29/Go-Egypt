Symbols of Ancient Egypt

Discover the rich symbolic language of the ancient Egyptians. These hieroglyphics and symbols were more than just writingâ€”they were sacred art that connected the physical world with the divine.

The Ankh: Symbol of Life

The ankh represents life and immortality. Egyptian gods were often portrayed carrying it by its loop, or bearing one in each hand, arms crossed over their chest.

The Eye of Horus: Divine Protection

The Eye of Horus symbolizes protection, royal power, and good health. It was one of the most common amulets in ancient Egypt.

The Scarab: Rebirth and Regeneration

Representing the cycle of rebirth and the morning sun, the scarab beetle was associated with the god Khepri who rolled the sun across the sky each day.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about traveling to Egypt.

The best time to visit Egypt is from October to April when temperatures are cooler. December and January are peak tourist months. Summer (May to September) can be extremely hot, especially in Upper Egypt, but you'll find fewer crowds and lower prices.

Most visitors to Egypt need a visa. Many nationalities can obtain a visa on arrival at Egyptian airports or apply for an e-visa online before traveling. It's recommended to check the latest visa requirements based on your nationality before planning your trip.

Egypt is generally safe for tourists, especially in the main tourist areas. Like any destination, it's important to stay aware of your surroundings, follow local advice, and respect cultural customs. We provide guides and support throughout your journey to ensure a safe and enjoyable experience.

Egypt is a conservative country, so modest dress is appreciated, especially when visiting religious sites. Lightweight, breathable clothing that covers shoulders and knees is recommended. For women, carrying a scarf is useful for mosque visits. In tourist resorts like Sharm El Sheikh, regular swimwear is acceptable by the pool or beach.

The Egyptian Pound (EGP) is the official currency. ATMs are widely available in cities and tourist areas. Credit cards are accepted at most hotels and larger establishments, but it's good to carry some cash for smaller vendors, tipping, and markets.
